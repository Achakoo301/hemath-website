About HemAth

Empowering displaced Kashmiri women through strength, heritage, and opportunity

HemAth’s story is rooted in resilience, cultural preservation, and the belief that when women are empowered, communities can rise with them.

Our Story

From displacement to renewed purpose

In 1990, when the world turned upside down for Kashmiri Pandits, families lost more than just their homes. They lost livelihoods, stability, and for many women, their dreams.

Yet like the mighty chinar tree, the community refused to be uprooted.

In 2018, five Kashmiri women—Dr. Shakun Malik, Dr. Rajesh Mattoo, Reena Moza, Renu Tickoo Wokhlu, and Surabhi Bhan Kotru—came together with a shared vision: to recognize the untapped potential of displaced women and create pathways for dignity, independence, and hope.

Why HemAth Exists

Strength is built into our name

Named after the Kashmiri word for “strength,” HemAth is dedicated to supporting displaced Kashmiri women by preserving cultural heritage and promoting economic independence.

Through handmade craft, community initiatives, mentorship, and cultural storytelling, HemAth helps transform adversity into meaningful opportunity.

Mission & Vision

What drives HemAth

Preserve Heritage

Protect and promote Kashmiri cultural traditions through craft, design, and storytelling.

Foster Independence

Support women through skill development and economic opportunity that can create lasting stability.

Build Resilience

Strengthen families and communities by investing in women’s growth, confidence, and leadership.

EmpowerHer Champions

The backbone of HemAth’s mission

At the heart of HemAth’s work are dedicated volunteers, supporters, mentors, and community champions.

Their leadership and commitment help ensure that HemAth’s vision becomes a lived reality for displaced Kashmiri women and their families.

Looking Ahead

A mission-led e-commerce future

HemAth’s long-term goal is to create a simple, welcoming product-purchase experience within HemAth.org, allowing visitors to browse and buy products like shawls, jewelry, and tea with ease while directly supporting the mission.
