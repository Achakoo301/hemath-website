Our Story

From displacement to empowerment

In 1990, when the world turned upside down for Kashmiri Pandits, entire communities were displaced from their homes, losing not only their livelihoods but also their sense of belonging.

In 2018, five Kashmiri women came together with a shared vision—to transform adversity into opportunity. They recognized the untapped potential of displaced women and believed that empowering them could uplift entire families and communities.

HemAth, named after the Kashmiri word for “strength,” was created to preserve cultural heritage while fostering economic independence and resilience among Kashmiri women.
